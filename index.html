Amanda West

Ph.D. Candidate, Georgia Institute of Technology

I am a Ph.D. candidate in the School of Electrical and Computer Engineering at the Georgia Institute of Technology, where I am advised by Dr. Daniel K. Molzahn in the Grid Optimization and Algorithms Laboratory (GOAL) Lab.

My research focuses on developing frameworks and power system optimization problems to enhance energy fairness, economic development, and resilience in under-resourced communities. At the intersection of power systems optimization and fairness, I work on modeling power system networks to assess the affordability and resilience of energy systems. I am passionate about bridging the gap between technical innovation and social impact in energy infrastructure.

I am privileged for my work to be supported by the NSF Graduate Research Fellowship Program (GRFP), the GEM Fellowship, the Pathbreakers Fellowship, and the Georgia Tech President's Fellowship.
